# Codex Context for Field Note Civic Labs

## Business Identity

Field Note Civic Labs is a small independent consulting practice based in Vancouver, BC.

The practice helps people and small organizations turn scattered records, paperwork, files, emails, spreadsheets, PDFs, public data, and working knowledge into practical AI-ready workflows. The public promise is not generic AI strategy. It is calm, concrete help getting control of messy information so AI can be useful, checkable, and manageable.

## Core Position

Use this positioning as the anchor:

> Field Note helps people turn scattered records, paperwork, and working knowledge into practical AI-ready workflows.

Shorter variant:

> Practical AI help for records, paperwork, and workflows.

## Commercial Purpose

The business should read as useful to paying customers who have too much scattered information and not enough capacity to organize, search, summarize, or reuse it.

Primary buyers or users may include:

- small business owners and independent professionals
- community-minded organizations with records and workflow problems
- people handling document-heavy administration, intake, review, or decision support
- local or civic contexts where official records, public data, or policy material need to be tracked
- teams that feel pressure to use AI but do not know where to start safely

Avoid making the site sound like:

- a generic AI agency
- a broad digital transformation consultancy
- a large civic-tech nonprofit
- a legal advice service
- a venture-scale SaaS company with inflated traction claims

## Evidence From Workspace

The identity is supported by these local project patterns:

- HR working knowledge tool: disorganized HR material converted into an AI-friendly working environment for a real task.
- Civic Early Warning: address-specific monitoring of official Vancouver municipal records, source-backed briefings, trust/provenance notes, and noise suppression.
- CRT Claim Assistant: evidence organization, exhibit indexing, transcript indexing, assertion-to-record mapping, and careful drafting support.
- Financial statement workflow: statement PDFs and category records processed into cleaner review and reuse workflows.
- Document intake tests: local file watching, classification, routing, and change logging.

When summarizing these, translate technical features into business outcomes:

- "normalize records" becomes "make scattered material easier to search and review"
- "source-backed summaries" becomes "summaries that can be checked"
- "evidence maps" becomes "fewer lost facts in document-heavy work"
- "AI tests" becomes "AI use that can be bounded, repeated, and corrected"
- "prototype" becomes "early result" when speaking to nontechnical visitors

## Site Messaging Rules

- Lead with the problem and buyer outcome, not with AI tooling.
- AI should appear as the immediate follow-up: useful once information is organized enough.
- Keep claims modest and accurate. Do not claim employees, clients, certifications, funding, legal status, registered nonprofit status, or production scale unless the files explicitly prove it.
- Use "working session", "early result", "small build", "workflow", "field note", "lightweight tool", or "consulting practice" where appropriate.
- Prefer concrete nouns: records, paperwork, files, emails, PDFs, spreadsheets, notes, decisions, workflows, summaries, indexes, intake, briefings.
- Make the work feel manageable to people who are anxious or skeptical about AI.
- Mention "not legal advice" around dispute, evidence, bylaw, or tribunal-adjacent work.
- Avoid vague phrases such as "empowering communities", "transforming democracy", "revolutionizing workflows", "unlocking insights", "jump on the AI bandwagon", or "AI-powered everything".

## Recommended Site Structure

1. Hero: records/paperwork/workflow problem first, AI as practical follow-up.
2. Help: how Field Note untangles records and maps real workflows.
3. Working session: concrete two-hour starting offer.
4. Method: information design, small early results, human-owned decisions.
5. Field Notes: applied examples paired with actual outcomes.
6. About/contact: small independent practice, Vancouver location, direct email.

## Tone

Plain, relatable, professional, and calm. The site should feel like a credible independent operator with deep competence in messy information workflows and practical AI use.

Use short sentences. Prefer clarity over polish. The visitor should quickly understand:

- what Field Note helps with
- why the work starts with their own material
- how AI becomes useful without becoming intimidating
- what a first engagement could look like
- what to ask about next

## Implementation Guidance

This repo is a static HTML/CSS site. Keep changes in `index.html`, `styles.css`, and documentation unless the user asks for a framework migration.

- check `git status --short`
- preserve user changes already present in the worktree

After editing:

- verify the site opens locally
- check mobile layout around 375px width and desktop around 1440px width
- confirm all text fits in cards/buttons
