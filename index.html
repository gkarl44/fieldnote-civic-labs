How Field Note helps

Build a working capability, not a one-off answer.

Many small teams use AI to draft isolated outputs. Field Note helps turn that same energy into a repeatable workflow: clear inputs, structured state, judgment criteria, reviewed recommendations, and reusable instructions.

Define the Entity

Identify what the work actually tracks: customers, issues, assets, obligations, topics, cases, projects, or something more specific.

Create the Working Record

Set Review Criteria

New program

AI Working Kit for small teams.

A guided working session or short program where your team turns one recurring workflow into a reusable AI-assisted process. The aim is not to turn everyone into software developers. It is to make one real piece of work clearer, repeatable, and easier to improve.

What the program covers

  • Choose one recurring workflow and the entity it tracks.
  • Gather the files, notes, records, examples, and source systems around it.
  • Normalize the material into a reviewable working record or queue.
  • Practice prompting, correction, prioritization, and human review.
  • Leave with one reusable workflow pattern your team can keep improving.

The shift

Use AI as a workflow engine, not only a generator.

The useful pattern is bigger than "write me an email." It is a way to collect evidence, maintain state, prioritize, recommend actions, and produce outputs with human review. In practice, that feels closer to a junior analyst than a chatbot.

The entity changes by domain: accounts, issues, assets, obligations, topics, experiments, customers, projects, or cases. Once those entities have state, the workflow can improve over time.

The reusable pattern

Start by naming the item your workflow tracks, then apply the same cycle.

1 Messy inputs

Systems, files, emails, notes, records, meetings, and examples.

2 Working record

Facts, sources, dates, status, contradictions, and open questions.

3 Priority queue

Ranked items based on clear criteria and current state.

4 Reviewed actions

Recommended next steps that people approve, reject, or modify.

5 Reusable skill

Instructions, prompts, templates, or tools that can be run again.

Start small

Begin with one real workflow.

Bring a recurring problem, a few examples, and the material around it. The point is not a slide deck. The point is to map the workflow, identify the entity with state, and define one useful next step.

What We Look At

Records, files, inboxes, spreadsheets, forms, public data, internal notes, recurring decisions, and the places where work gets delayed or repeated.

What You Leave With

A plain map of the workflow, a first structured view or queue, a short list of realistic AI opportunities, and one recommended first build or cleanup step.

What We Avoid

Hype, vague strategy, and tools that make sensitive or important work harder to check. Some problems need cleanup before automation.

Where It Can Lead

An AI-ready workspace, a reusable skill, a searchable knowledge base, a workflow helper, a lightweight internal tool, or ongoing support if the early result proves useful.

Pattern

Think in entities with state, not loose documents.

Field Note brings practical AI tutoring, information design, and small-tool thinking to messy real-world work. The goal is to make AI feel usable by turning repeated work into a named capability your team can run again.

  • Start with the entity your work tracks: account, issue, asset, topic, project, or case.
  • Maintain a reviewable record of facts, sources, dates, status, and decisions.
  • Rank work by clear criteria instead of reacting to the latest message.
  • Generate recommended actions, not final decisions.
  • Keep important review and approval steps human-owned.
  • Capture the working pattern as a reusable skill.

Field Notes

Applied work tied to entities, state, and review.

These are not inflated case studies. They are field notes from practical work and working models: some client-adjacent, some experimental, all focused on turning scattered inputs into a structure that supports review and next action.

HR Working Knowledge Tool

Disorganized HR material became a working environment for a real task: gather the source material, normalize the facts, propose useful next steps, and keep people in review.

Civic Records Monitor

Public permit and civic records were shaped into a briefing queue, making it easier to track changes, filter noise, summarize what mattered, and keep links back to official sources.

Evidence and Paperwork Assistant

Scattered documents, emails, notes, timelines, and exhibits were organized around issues with sources, dates, contradictions, and review status. This is information organization, not legal advice.

Financial Statement Workflow

Statement PDFs and category records were processed into reviewable assets, obligations, categories, and upcoming decisions, reducing the amount of manual checking needed each time.

Document Intake and Sorting

Local workflow tests watch incoming files, classify material, route documents to review folders, and log what changed, turning intake into a repeatable capability.
