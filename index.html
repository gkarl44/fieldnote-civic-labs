What we build

Briefings, checks, indexes, reviews, and research loops for teams with scattered records.

The work usually starts with public systems, forms, inboxes, spreadsheets, transcripts, or policy documents. The output stays narrow, source-linked, and simple enough to review.

Public Record Briefings

Convert official records and local datasets into short updates with source links, plain-language summaries, and notes about why each item was included.

Policy and Rule Checks

Turn procurement, bylaw, or internal policy scenarios into structured checks with clear reasons, reviewed inputs, and document-style outputs.

Document Intake and Evidence Mapping

Organize files, transcripts, messages, exhibits, and claims so important facts can be found, checked, and connected to source material.

AI Workflow Reviews

Identify where AI can safely help, where it should not be used, and how to test prompts, outputs, and source-tracing before relying on results.

Audience and Marketability Research

Track public signals, peer examples, language patterns, and practical objections so positioning decisions are based on reviewed evidence instead of guesswork.

Method

Source first. Narrow scope. Human-checkable output.

AI is used to help summarize, sort, compare, and test material after records are gathered. It should not replace source retrieval, deterministic checks, or human judgment.

  • Start with records, not prompts.
  • Filter before summarizing.
  • Link claims back to source material.
  • Flag weak matches and uncertainty.
  • Keep AI inside bounded review steps.
  • Leave final decisions to people.

Working Models

Applied work with public records, documents, and careful review.

These projects are working models for a single pattern: retrieve or organize records first, keep source material close, summarize narrowly, and leave important judgments to a person.

Civic Early Warning

Address-specific monitoring of official municipal records. The working Vancouver slice turns building permit data into weekly, source-backed briefings with radius filtering, change detection, why-shown notes, and provenance warnings.

Procurement Policy Checks

A rule-backed workflow for checking procurement scenarios against policy requirements. It emphasizes explicit inputs, yes-or-no determinations, short reasons, and output that can be reviewed later.

Evidence and Transcript Mapping

A document-heavy workflow for organizing claims, exhibits, transcripts, and supporting records. It is not legal advice; it is evidence organization and source-linked drafting support.

Document Intake and Sorting

Local workflow tests for watching incoming files, classifying material, routing documents to review folders, and logging what changed, with stricter handling for sensitive or unclear records.

Marketing Research Agent

An early workflow concept for continued marketability research: gather public-facing examples, compare audience language, surface objections, and prepare notes a person can use to refine service positioning.

About

Independent, lightweight, and practical.

The practice works with civic, nonprofit, strata, research, and small-business contexts where information is scattered and time is limited. The emphasis is on careful research, simple software, and documentation that a real person can maintain. Public-record tools should prefer official sources, show why an item appears, and make uncertainty visible. Fieldnote Civic Labs does not provide legal advice. Dispute-adjacent and tribunal-adjacent work is limited to evidence organization, source mapping, drafting support, and workflow design.
